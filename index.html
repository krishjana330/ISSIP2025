About the Conference

Welcome to CSMAI 2026

The International Conference on Complex System Modelling and Artificial Intelligence (CSMAI 2026) aims to bring together researchers, academicians, and industry professionals to discuss the latest advancements, trends, and challenges in the fields of complex systems and artificial intelligence.

This conference serves as a global platform for the exchange of innovative ideas, emerging methodologies, and transformative research that bridge theoretical foundations with real-world applications.

Call for Papers

Researchers, academicians, and practitioners are invited to submit original and unpublished research papers.

Detailed Topics

Complex System Modelling

  • Nonlinear dynamic systems
  • Computational modeling and simulation
  • Multi-agent systems
  • Network theory and analysis
  • Self-organization and emergent behavior

Artificial Intelligence

  • Deep learning and neural networks
  • Reinforcement learning and optimization
  • Explainable AI (XAI) and trustworthy AI
  • Cognitive computing and hybrid intelligence
  • Natural language processing

Applications

  • Smart cities and intelligent transportation
  • IoT-based systems and digital twins
  • Cybersecurity and intelligent threat detection
  • AI in healthcare, finance, and education
  • Sustainable development and green AI

Submission Guidelines

  • The authors are invited to submit the Full papers of the Research/Review Papers.
  • The papers will be peer-reviewed and only the accepted and registered papers will be considered for presentation and publication.
  • All papers must be submitted online via Microsoft CMT Portal: Microsoft CMT
  • Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished research papers that are not being considered for publication elsewhere.
  • The length of each paper should be 12 pages, including figures, tables, references.
  • The review process will be double-blind.
  • Authors must ensure submissions are free from plagiarism, self-plagiarism and AI Detector (Similarity Limit is Less than 10%, 0% AI content, and single source is maximum to 2%).
  • To prepare a paper in Springer format, authors need to adhere to specific guidelines. Paper is in a Springer template using the following template only:
Word Template Download LATEX Template Download

"The Microsoft CMT service was used for managing the peer-reviewing process for this conference. This service was provided for free by Microsoft and they bore all expenses, including costs for Azure cloud services as well as for software development and support. All submissions will be thoroughly peer-reviewed by experts based on originality, significance and clarity."
